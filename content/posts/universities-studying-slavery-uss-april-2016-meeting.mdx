On Friday April 8, 2016, Washington and Lee University in Lexington, Virginia, hosted the University of Virginia President's Commission on Slavery and the University's Universities Studying Slavery (USS) consortium. The consortium met just two days after Washington and Lee University's African American History Working Group erected a marker in honor of enslaved laborers.
Washington and Lee President Ruscio's remarks at the introduction of the historical marker can be read and listened to here: [http://www.wlu.edu/presidents-office/about-president-ruscio/speeches/remarks-at-the-historical-marker-introduction](http://www.wlu.edu/presidents-office/about-president-ruscio/speeches/remarks-at-the-historical-marker-introduction)

The tour was followed by a panel entitled *Memorials, Markers, and Monuments to Enslaved Laborers: Process, Progress, and Perceptions.*
<Image src={imag3017} alt="" />
Representatives from the University of Virginia, the College of William and Mary, Washington and Lee University, Roanoke College, and Georgetown University highlighted the specific challenges they have faced in addressing the multifaceted processes of memorialization. After a rich discussion, the attendees broke into smaller groups and discussed the political issues surrounding memorialization and the current movement to remove confederate memorials.
USS will meet again this summer at the College of William and Mary.
